Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is an authentic Roman pasta dish. It is said that the term "carbonara" originated from coal miners who would make this dish underground, where bits of charcoal that resemble carbon would fall onto the pasta, which would then later be substituted with ground black pepper. Spaghetti and linguine are typically used, but rigatoni and penne are great alternatives.

Contrary to popular belief, its creamy texture is not derrived from any form of cream, but is in fact the result of a rigatorious emulsification from the cheese, fat rendered from the pork cheeks, starchy pasta water and egg yolks only.

    Ingredients
  • Linguine
  • Pecorino romano
  • Guanciale
  • Egg yolks
  • Black peppercorns
  • Water
    Steps
  1. Get a pot of water boiling.
  2. Slice up guanciale into thin chunks. Grate pecorino romano finely and set aside.
  3. Heat up a pan to medium heat and toast black peppercorns lightly.
  4. Remove the peppercorns when a strong pungent smell is released and add them into a grinder.
  5. In a new cold pan, add in guanciale at low heat. Render as much fat before the guanciale is browned.
  6. Remove the pan from heat and strain the guanciale. Reserve the rendered fat.
  7. Add linguine to the pot and cook until al dente.
  8. In a mixing bowl, add in egg yolks, rendered fat, pecorino romano, cracked black pepper, guanciale, pasta water and mix well.
  9. Strain the linguine, add it to the mixing bowl and combine well.
  10. Plate the pasta and garnish with more guanciale, pecorino romano and black pepper. Serve while warm.
