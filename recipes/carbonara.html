Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is an authentic Roman pasta dish, traditionally made using pecorino romano, guanciale and egg yolks. Spaghetti and linguine are typically used, but rigatoni and penne are great alternatives.

Contrary to popular belief, its creamy texture is not derrived from any form of cream, but is in fact the result of a rigatorious emulsification from the cheese, fat rendered from the pork cheeks, starchy pasta water and egg yolks only.

    Ingredients
  • Pasta of choice
  • Pecorino cheese
  • Guanciale
  • Egg yolks
  • Black pepper
  • Water
